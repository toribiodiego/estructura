### Domain Context [-> Usefulness]

This chart extends the R06 comparison by breaking down AU/NZ standards
into resolution-based tiers. Under the AU/NZ 2013 MEPS framework:

- Higher resolution displays (8MPx UHD) are allowed more power than
  lower resolution (1MPx SD) for the same screen area
- The MEPS regulation only applied to screens smaller than 76cm diagonal
  (2458 cm2 area), explaining why the AU/NZ lines terminate

large-format displays (>76cm), which are increasingly common (large TVs,
digital signage). The EU standards have no such size limitation.

The chart shows that for screens below the 76cm threshold, the AU/NZ
UHD 8MPx allowance is extremely high (~95W at cutoff), while EU
standards for the same area would allow only ~30-40W depending on
the regulation.

### Domain Context [-> Usefulness]

The Australian Energy Rating label is mandated by the E3 (Equipment Energy
Efficiency) program, a joint initiative of Australian and New Zealand
governments and industry. The label system uses a star rating (1-10) where
more stars indicate better efficiency. Labels with 7+ stars carry the
"Super Efficiency" designation with a distinct diagonal banner.

The 199 kWh/year figure represents annual energy consumption based on 10
hours of daily use, tested per AS/NZS 62087.2.2. The label warns that
retail display settings may differ from the tested mode. The
energyrating.gov.au website provides a comparison tool for consumers.

This is the more efficient of the two energy labels in the document
(doc19-R10 shows the Standard label at 485 kWh/year for the same model).
