### Domain Context [-> Retrieval Value]

This is an official NASA visualization from the Goddard Space Flight Center
Scientific Visualization Studio (SVS), cataloged as visualization a014718.
It shows all active heliophysics missions as of December 2025.

Heliophysics is the study of the Sun and its effects on the solar system,
including solar wind, space weather, magnetospheric physics, and
Sun-Earth interactions. NASA's Heliophysics Division operates a fleet of
spacecraft distributed across the inner solar system to study these phenomena.

Key mission categories visible:
- **Solar observation:** Parker Solar Probe (closest approach to Sun), Solar
  Orbiter, SDO, SOHO, STEREO
- **Solar wind/heliosphere:** ACE, Wind, IMAP, IBEX, Voyager
- **Magnetosphere/ionosphere:** THEMIS, MMS, GOLD, TIMED, ICON, AWE
- **Multi-mission campaigns:** TRACERS, PUNCH (newer missions in formation)
- **Mars surface:** RAD on Curiosity rover (radiation measurement)

The spatial positioning of spacecraft in the diagram conveys their approximate
operational regions in the Sun-Earth system, from the inner corona (Parker)
to the outer heliosphere (Voyager) and Mars.
