Environmental Impact Assessment -- Riverside Development

1. Introduction

This report presents the environmental impact assessment for the
proposed Riverside Development project in Clark County. The assessment
covers land use changes, water quality projections, and habitat impact
across the 240-hectare site.

The study period spans January 2024 through December 2025, with field
surveys conducted during three seasonal windows.

2. Land Use Analysis

Current land use breaks down as follows: 62% agricultural (primarily
row crops), 24% grassland, 8% forested riparian corridor, and 6%
existing impervious surface from the former industrial facility.

3. Water Quality Projections

Baseline sampling at six monitoring stations established mean total
phosphorus at 0.08 mg/L and total suspended solids at 22 mg/L during
dry-weather conditions.

[IMAGE: img-p003-01 | images/crops/p003-01.png]
  [Placeholder: figure on page 3, region 1]

The model predicts a 15% increase in peak stormwater volume for the
2-year return period event, controlled to pre-development rates by
the regional detention facility shown in the site plan.

Post-construction monitoring will continue for five years at the same
six stations to verify model predictions against observed conditions.
